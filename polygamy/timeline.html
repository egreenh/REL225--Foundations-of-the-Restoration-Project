ca. 1831

Learning of Polygamy

Joseph initially learns about plural marriage via divine revelation

ca. 1835

First Plural Marriage

Joseph Smith marries his first plural wife, Fanny Alger.

July 12, 1843

Revelation

The revelation that later becomes D&C 132 is recorded by Joseph Smith.

June 27, 1844

Joseph Smith's Death

Joseph Smith is killed in Carthage Jail.

August 29, 1852

Public Announcement regarding Polygamy

Orson Pratt makes an announcment at General Conference regarding plural marriage being a practice of the Church.

1862

Morrill Anti-Bigamy Act

The United States Congress passed this act in order to criminalize polygamy, overturning the laws in Utah.

1876

D&C 132

The revelation recorded by Joseph Smith regarding plural marriage is cannoized as D&C 132.

1879

Reynolds v. United States

The Supreme Court decides that the practice of polygamy is not protected by the First Amendment.

1882

Edmunds Act

The United States Congress passes the Edmunds Act, further restricting the practice of polygamy by making it a federal misdemeanor.

ca. 1882-1887

Into Hiding

Many Church leaders are forced into hiding in order to escape prosecution after the passing of the Edmunds Act.

1887

Edmunds-Tucker Act

The United States Congress passes the Edmunds-Tucker Act, revoking the legal status of the Church, seizing their assets and land, and even stripping Utah women of their voting rights.

May 19, 1890

Supreme Court Ruling on Edmunds-Tucker Act

The United States Supreme Court rules that the Edmunds-Tucker Act is constitutional.

October 6, 1890

Manifesto

President Wilford Woodruff issues the Manifesto, which was later added to the Doctrine and Covenants as Official Declaration 1, effectively ending plural marriage as a teaching in the Church.
